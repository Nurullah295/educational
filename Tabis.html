Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)
1. Why is Tabis (Bisoprolol) suitable for Asthmatic hypertensive patients?
Ans: Beta 2 receptor located in Lung which is responsible for bronchial vasodilation. Tabis (Bisoprolol) is highly selective to Beta 1 receptor thus it does not hamper the function of Beta 2 receptor in lung. That's why Tabis (Bisoprolol) is suitable for asthmatic hypertensive patients.
2. Why is Tabis suitable drug for smoker hypertensive patients?
Ans: Smoking increases the release of epinephrine and it stimulates the activity of Beta 1 receptor which is responsible for increasing heart rate and blood pressure. Tabis is highly selective beta 1 blocker which works in heart and kidney and blocks the activity of Beta 1 receptor to control blood pressure.
3. Why is Tabis (Bisoprolol) better than Atenolol in smoker hypertensive patients?
Ans: Tabis (Bisoprolol) and Atenolol both are beta 1 blocker but Tabis is highly & more selective (19 Fold) than Atenolol (5 Fold) to beta 1 receptor (located in heart and kidney) which blocks the activity of Beta 1 receptor thus effectively control BP of smoker hypertensive patients more than Atenolol.
4. Why is Tabis (Bisoprolol) better than Metoprolol in smoker hypertensive patients?
Ans: Tabis (Bisoprolol) and Metoprolol both are beta 1 blocker but Tabis is highly & more selective (19 Fold) than Metoprolol (6 Fold) to beta 1 receptor (located in heart and kidney) which blocks the activity of Beta 1 receptor thus effectively control BP of smoker hypertensive patients more than Metoprolol.
