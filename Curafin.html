    Terminology
    • Dermatophytes:
    Dermatophytes are fungi. These fungi can cause superficial infections of the skin, hair and nails.

    • Tinea:
    Any fungal skin disease is called Tinea. For example: Ringworm

    • Tinea corporis:
    It is also known as ringworm, a superficial fungal infection (dermatophytosis) of the arms and legs, especially on glabrous skin.

    • Tinea pedis:
    Athlete's foot (tinea pedis) is a fungal infection that usually begins between the toes.

    Tinea cruris:
    Tinea cruris is a fungal infection that causes a red and itchy rash in warm and moist areas of the body.

    • Tinea versicolor:
    The fungus interferes with the normal pigmentation of the skin, resulting in small, discolored patches. It most commonly affects the trunk and shoulders.

    • Tinea inguinalis:
    Superficial fungal infection of the groins.

    • Tinea manuum:
    Fungal infection of the hands.
