ENT & Head Neck Diseases

1. Acute bacterial exacerbation of chronic bronchitis:
Acute Exacerbations of Chronic Bronchitis (AECB), is a sudden worsening of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), including shortness of breath due to bacterial infection of the lung.

2. Acute bacterial exacerbation of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease:
Acute bacterial exacerbation of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease is a sudden worsening of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) symptoms including shortness of breath, quantity and color of phlegm which is triggered by infection caused by bacteria that typically lasts for several days.
3. Acute bacterial sinusitis:
Acute bacterial sinusitis (ABS) is an infection of both your nasal cavity and sinuses caused by bacteria.
4. Acute otitis media:
Acute otitis media (AOM) is a painful type of ear infection. It occurs when the area behind the eardrum called the middle ear becomes inflamed and infected.
5. Community-acquired pneumonia:
Pneumonia is a type of lung infection which can cause breathing problems and other symptoms. In community-acquired pneumonia (CAP), you get infected in a community setting.
6. Covid Pneumonia:
Most people who get COVID-19 have mild or moderate symptoms like coughing, fever and shortness of breath. But some who catch the new corona virus get severe pneumonia in both lungs. COVID-19 pneumonia is a serious illness that can be deadly.
7. Maxillary sinusitis:
Maxillary sinusitis is inflammation of the maxillary sinuses. The maxillary sinuses are located under the eyes. It occurs due to inflammation of the para-nasal sinuses caused by bacterial infection. Common symptoms include facial pain or pressure, nasal congestion or discharge and smell sensation reduces.
8. Otitis media:
Otitis media is a group of inflammatory diseases of the middle ear. Otitis media occurs as a result of a cold, sore throat or respiratory infection. The most common symptoms include ear pain, feeling of fullness in the ear, fever, hearing loss.
9. Pharyngitis:
Pharyngitis and tonsillitis are infections in the throat that cause inflammation. If the tonsils are primarily affected, it is called tonsillitis. If the throat is primarily affected, it is called pharyngitis.
10. Pneumonia:
Pneumonia is an infection in one or both lungs. It's usually caused by a bacterial infection. It can also be caused by a virus, such as coronavirus (COVID-19). Common symptoms include cough, fever, sweating and shaking chills, shortness of breath. Main causative pathogens- Streptococcus pneumonia
11. Respiratory tract infection (RTI):
It is defined as any infectious disease of the upper or lower respiratory tract. Upper respiratory tract infections (URTIS) include the laryngitis, pharyngitis, tonsillitis, sinusitis and acute otitis media. Lower respiratory tract infections (LRTIS) include acute bronchitis, bronchiolitis and pneumonia.
12. Tonsillitis:
Tonsillitis is inflammation of the tonsils. Symptoms may include sore throat, fever, enlargement of the tonsils, trouble swallowing.
