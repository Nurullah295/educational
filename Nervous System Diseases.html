Nervous System Diseases

1. ADHD:
Attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) is a mental health disorder that can cause above-normal levels of hyperactive and impulsive behaviors.
2. Anxiety:
Anxiety is a feeling of unease, such as worry or fear that can be mild or severe.
3. Burning mouth syndrome:
Burning mouth syndrome is a chronic or recurrent burning in the mouth without an obvious cause. This discomfort may affect the tongue, gums, lips, inside of cheeks, roof of your mouth (palate) or widespread areas of your whole mouth.
4. Depression:
Depression is a common and serious medical illness that negatively affects how you feel, the waves think and how you act. Depression causes feelings of marked & persistent sadness and/or a loss of interest in activities you once enjoyed.
5. Diabetic Neuropathy:
Diabetic neuropathy is a type of nerve damage that can occur if you have diabetes. High sugar (glucose) can injure nerves throughout the body. Diabetic neuropathy most often damages nerves in legs and feet.
6. Fibromyalgia:
Fibromyalgia is a disorder characterized by widespread musculoskeletal pain accompanied by fatigue, sleep, memory and mood issues.
7. Growth Retardation:
Growth retardation occurs when fetus doesn't develop at a normal rate. It's widely referred to as intrauterine growth restriction (IUGR).
8. Lumbago:
Lumbago is a general term used to describe pain in the lower back.
9. MADD:
MADD (Mixed anxiety-depressive disorder) is defining as having both anxiety and depressive symptoms of limited and equal intensity accompanied by at least some autonomic features. Autonomic features are involuntary physical symptoms usually caused by an overactive nervous system, such as panic attacks or intestinal distress 10. Migraine:
It is a recurring type of headache that can cause severe throbbing or a pulsing sensation, usually on one side of the head and is often accompanied by nausea and disturbed vision.
11. Myalgia:
Myalgia describes muscle aches and pain, which can involve ligaments, tendons and fascia, the soft tissues that connect muscles, bones and organs.
12. Neuralgia:
Burning sensation causing stabbing & severe pain.
13. Neuritis:
Inflammation in nerves & pain in neurons causing loss of nerve function.
14. Neuropathic Pain from Spinal Cord Injury:
This kind of pain is caused by abnormal communications between the brain and the nerves damaged by spinal cord injury. The majority of people with spinal cord Injury report that they have chronic pain. Words such as burning, stabbing or tingling are often used to describe neuropathic pain by patients, but neuropathic pain varies a great deal from person to person.
15. Palpitations:
Heart palpitation is feelings of having a fast-beating, fluttering or pounding heart. Stress, exercise, medication or, rarely, a medical condition can trigger them.
16. Panic attacks:
Panic attacks, which are sudden feelings of terror when there is no real danger. Symptoms may include palpitations, sweating, shaking, shortness of breath, numbness, or a feeling that something terrible is going to happen.
17. Panic disorder:
Panic disorder is an anxiety disorder characterized by reoccurring unexpected panic attacks.
18. Partial Seizure or Epilepsy:
Seizure is a sudden, uncontrolled electrical disturbance in the brain. It can cause changes in behavior, movements or feelings, and in levels of consciousness. Epilepsy is a condition that causes multiple seizures, and the seizures can be of any type. Partial seizure is a type of seizure associated with epilepsy.
19. Post herpetic neuralgia:
Post herpetic neuralgia is the most common complication of shingles. The condition affects nerve fibers and skin, causing burning pain that lascs long after the rash and blisters of shingles disappear.
20. REM sleep behavior disorder:
Rapid eye movement (REM) sleep behavior disorder is a sleep disorder in which you physically act out vivid, often unpleasant dreams with vocal sounds and sudden, often violent arm and leg movements during REM sleep - sometimes called dream-enacting behavior.
21. Sciatica:
Pain affecting the back hip and outer side of the leg caused by compression of spinal nerve.
22. Seizure:
A seizure, formally known as an epileptic seizure, is a period of symptoms due to abnormally excessive neuronal activity in the brain.
23. Trigeminal neuralgia:
Trigeminal neuralgia is a chronic pain condition that affects the trigeminal nerves, which carry sensation from face to brain & leading to facial paralysis.

Depression:
According to WHO, Depression is characterized by persistent sadness and a lack of interest or pleasure in previously rewarding or enjoyable activities. It can also disturb sleep and appetite; tiredness and poor concentration are commonly reported.

Depression is more dangerous than we think!
Depression not only alters a person’s social behavior but it also brings about changes in a person’s brain The effects of depression can be long-lasting or recurrent and can dramatically affect a person’s ability to function and live a rewarding life.

Sign & Symptoms of Depression
1. Feeling sad or having a depressed mood
2. Loss of interest or pleasure in activities once enjoyed
3. Changes in appetite — weight loss or gain unrelated to dieting
4. Trouble sleeping or sleeping too much
5. Loss of energy or increased fatigue
6. Increase in purposeless physical activity (e.g.. band-wringing or pacing) or slowed movements and speech (actions observable by others)
7. Feeling worthless or guilty
8. Difficulty thinking, concentrating or making decisions
9. Thoughts of death or suicide

Anxiety:
Anxiety is a normal and often healthy emotion. However, when a person regularly feels disproportionate levels of anxiety, it might become a medical disorder.

Sign & Symptoms of Anxiety
1. Restlessness
2. Excessive worrying
3. Difficulty in concentrating
4. Excessive carefulness
5. Irritability
6. Trouble falling or staying asleep
7. Some physical symptoms including fatigue. anorexia, tension headache. palpitation, short memory etc.
8. Lack of sexual desire
9. Over reaction
10. Experiencing gastrointestinal (GI) problems

Apathy:
Apathy is a lack of feeling, emotion, interest, and concern. An apathetic individual has an absence of interest in or concern about emotional, social, spiritual, philosophical and/or physical life and the world.

Psychogenic depression:
Psychogenic depression is a type of depression which arises from physical illnesses , emotional or mental stressors, or from psychological or psychiatric disorders.

Menopausal depression:
Depression after menopause is known as menopausal depression. This depression takes place due to hormonal disturbance prevails after cessation of menstruation

Dysphoria:
In a psychiatric context. dysphoria may accompany depression, anxiety, or agitation. It can also refer to a state of not being comfortable in one’s current body.
