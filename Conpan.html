GABA : The Sleep Doctor!
Gamma Aminobutyric Acid (GABA) is considered an inhibitory neurotransrmitter because it blocks or inhibits certain brain signals & decreases hyper activity of nervous system.
When GABA attaches to a protein in brain known as a GABA receptor, it produces a calming effect. This can help to improve feelings of anxiety, stress, and fear. It may also help to prevent seizure.

Seizure disorders:
A seizure is a sudden, uncontrolled electrical disturbance in the brain. It can cause changes in your behavior, movements or feelings, and in levels of consciousness. If one has two or more seizures or a tendency to have recurrent seizures, he has epilepsy.

Panic Disorders:
Panic disorder is characterized by reoccurring unexpected panic attacks.
Panic attacks are sudden periods of intense fear that may include palpitations, sweating, shaking, shortness of breath, numbness, or a feeling that something terrible is going to happen.

Mood disorders:
Mood disorders are characterized by a serious change in mood that cause disruption to life activities. Though many different subtypes are recognized, three major states of mood disorders exist:
depressive,
manic, and
bipolar.

REM Sleep Behavior Disorder:
Rapid eye movement (REM) sleep behavior disorder is a sleep disorder in which you physically act out vivid, often unpleasant dreams with vocal sounds and sudden, often violent arm and leg movements during REM sleep— sometimes called dream-enacting behavior.

Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD):
PTSD is a mental health condition that’s triggered by a terrifying event either experiencing it or witnessing it. Symptoms may include flashbacks, nightmares and severe anxiety, as well as uncontrollable thoughts about the event.
