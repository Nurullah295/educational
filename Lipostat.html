Lipid:
Lipids are molecules that contain hydrocarbons and make up the building blocks of the structure and function of living cells.
Class of organic compounds that are fatty acids or their derivatives and are insoluble in water but soluble in organic solvents
Examples of lipids include fats, oils, waxes, certain vitamins, hormones and most of the non-protein membrane of cells.
• Triglycerides : Major energy source for cells
• Cholesterol : Cell growth, cell division, membrane repair, steroid hormone production
• Lipids : Transport of fat soluble vitamins

Cholesterol
• Cholesterol is a lipid which is produced by the liver.
• Cholesterol is vital for normal body function.
• Every cell in our body has cholesterol in its outer layer.
• Cholesterol is a waxy steroid and is transported in the blood plasma of all animals.
• Cholesterol transport by lipoproteins.
• Low Density Lipoproteins:
LDL transports cholesterol throughout body (Called Bad cholesterol).
• High Density Lipoproteins:
HDL removes excess cholesterol and carries it back to liver for degradation (Called Good cholesterol).

Lipoprotein:
Lipoproteins are molecules made of proteins and fat.
They carry cholesterol and similar substances through the blood.
Types of lipoprotein
• High density lipoproteins (HDL)
• Low density lipoproteins (LDL)
• Very low density lipoproteins (VLDL)
• Intermediate density lipoproteins (IDL)
• Chylomicrons

Good vs Bad Cholesterol
Cholesterol transport by lipoproteins
Low Density Lipoproteins (Bad Cholesterol): LDL transports cholesterol throughout body
High Density Lipoproteins (Good Cholesterol): HDL removes excess cholesterol and carries it back to liver for degradation

Disease Part:
Hyperlipidemia:
The presence of raised of lipids or lipoproteins in the blood.
Dyslipidemia:.
The presence of abnormal levels of lipids in the blood.
Mixed Dyslipidemia:
It is the combination of elevated LDL cholesterol and triglyceride and decreased HDL Cholesterol. It may occur with certain genetic disorders, diabetes mellitus, the metabolic syndrome, or the use of drugs such as immunosuppressive agents or protease inhibitors.
Hypertriglyceridemia:
It is a condition in which triglyceride levels are elevated. It is often caused or exacerbated by uncontrolled diabetes mellitus, obesity and sedentary habits. It is a risk factor for coronary artery disease (CAD).
Dysbetalipoproteinemia:
It is also called remnant removal disease. It is a rare lipid disorder characterized by high levels of blood cholesterol and triglycerides. Cholesterol levels: 300-600 mg/dL. Triglyceride Ievels>400 mg/dL and may exceed 1000 mg/dL.
Atherosclerosis:
Deposition of fatty substance inside the blood vessels
