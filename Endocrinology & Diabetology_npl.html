Diabetology & Endocrinology

1. Diabetes Mellitus:

A disease in which the body's ability to produce or respond to the hormone insulin is impaired, resulting in abnormal metabolism of carbohydrates and elevated levels of glucose in the blood.

2. Diabetic Diarrhoea:

Diabetic diarrhoea is a troublesome gastrointestinal complication of diabetes. This condition persists for several weeks to months and it frequently accompanies foecal incontinence.

3. Diabetic ketoacidosis:

Diabetic ketoacidosis is a serious complication of diabetes that occurs when body produces high levels of blood acids called ketones. The condition develops when body can't produce enough insulin.

4. Diabetic neuropathy:

Nerve damage caused by diabetes that leads to numbness and sometimes pain and weakness in the hands, arms, feet, and legs.

5. Gestational diabetes:

It is a condition in which a woman without diabetes develops high blood sugar levels during pregnancy.

6. Glucose:

Glucose is a type of sugar that all living organisms get from their foods and body uses it for energy. It travels through bloodstream to cells for producing energy.

7. Glycated haemoglobin (HbA1c):

It develops when haemoglobin binds with glucose in the blood becoming 'Glycated'. The level of glycated haemoglobin is increased in the red blood cells of people with poorly controlled diabetes mellitus. The normal level for glycated haemoglobin (HbA1c) is less than 7%.

8. Hypoglycaemia:

Hypoglycaemia is a condition in which blood sugar (glucose) level is lower than normal.

9. Insulin:

Insulin is a hormone produced from the beta cells of the Islets of Langerhans in pancreas. It helps move glucose into cells to be used as energy.
