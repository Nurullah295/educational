Respiratory Diseases

1. Asthma:
It is a long-term inflammatory disease of the airways of the lungs. It is characterized by variable symptoms such as wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness & cough.
2. COPD:
It is a chronic inflammatory lung disease that causes obstructed airflow from the lungs. Symptoms include breathing difficulty, cough, mucoid sputum production and wheezing. It includes chronic bronchitis & emphysema.
3. Dry cough:
Dry cough is one that does not produce any mucus or phlegm during coughing. However, persistent dry cough can become a big concern.
4. Exercise-induced bronchoconstriction:
It is the narrowing of the airways causing difficulty in moving air out of the lungs during exercise or physical activity.

7. Shortness of breath:
It is known medically as dyspnea, often described as an intense tightening in the chest, air hunger, difficulty in breathing, breathlessness or a feeling of suffocation.
8. Wheezing:
This is a continuous, coarse, whistling sound produced in the respiratory airways during breathing.
