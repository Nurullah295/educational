Stones form when the levels of uric acid in the urine is too high, and/or the urine is too acidic (pH level below 5.5) on a regular basis
• More common in men than in women
• Occur in people with gout

Uric Acid Kidney Stone
Symptoms
• Pain in the lower back sides, abdomen or groin
• Blood in the urine
• Nausea or vomiting
• Fever and chills

Treatment
• Drinking more water
• Urinary alkalization: Potassium citrate or sodium bicarbonate

Discomfort in Urinary Tract Infections (Burning of Urination)
• A burning sensation with urination can be caused by infectious and noninfectious conditions
• It also may occur due to decreased pH or acidic urine
• Women are more likely to develop than men

Symptoms
• Pain, Burning or Discomfort upon urination
• Urinary urgency
• Cloudy & Dark urine

Treatment
• Drinking more water
• Antibiotics or along with Urinary Alkaliniser

Acidosis (Renal tubular acidosis)
• RTA is a disease that occurs when the kidneys fail to excrete acids into the urine
• It may be caused by failure to reabsorb bicarbonate ions (Proximal tubule) or by secretion of hydrogen ions (distal tubule)

Symptoms
• Confusion, Fatigue
• Increased breathing rate
• Kidney stones
• Calcium deposited in kidneys
• Muscle weakness

Treatment
• Urinary Alkaliniser
• Diuretics

• Alkaliniser:
Agent that neutralizes acidity in blood (less acidic).
• Alkalization:
A process that lowers the amount of acid in blood.
• Acidosis:
It refers to an excess of acid in the blood
• Alkalinizing agents:
Alkalinizing agents are drugs used to manage disorders associated with low pH e.g. Acidosis.
• Uricosuric agents:
These are substances that increase the excretion of uric acid in the urine. e.g. Allopurinol, Probenecid.
