Diabetes:
Diabetes is a group of metabolic disorder in which persistent hyperglycaemia is caused
‣ by deficient insulin secretion or
‣ by resistance to the action of insulin

Glucose:
Glucose is a type of sugar that all living organism get from their foods, and body uses It for energy. It travels through bloodstream to cells for producing ENERGY.

Insulin:
Insulin is a hormon produced from the beta cells of the Islets of Langerhans in pancreas. It helps to move glucose into cells to be used as energy.

What is the role of Glucose & Insulin in body?
• Glucose produce energy from the cells
• Insulin is the key to open the cells

Diabetes is Manageable, But is not Curable To maintain optimum blood glucose level, Diabetic patients needs:
• Diet + Discipline + Drug (3D Theory)

Glycated Haemoglobin (HbA1C):
It develops when Haemoglobin bind with glucose in the blood becoming ‘Glycated’. The level of Glycated hemoglobin is increased in the red blood cells of persons with poorly controlled diabetes mellitus. The normal level for Glycated Haemoglobin is less than 7%.

Mechanism of Sulfonylureas (Glix)
Sulfonylureas reduce blood glucose level in two pissible ways.
• 1. They act on beta cells in the pancreas to release more insulin in to the blood stream
• 2. They also reduce Insulin resistance

Q. Write down the mechanism of action of Glix Tablet.
Ans:
‣ Gliclazide binds to the β cell sulfonyl urea receptor (SUR1).
‣ This binding subsequently blocks the ATP sensitive potassium channels.
‣ As a results decrease in potassium efflux leads to depolarization of the β cells.
‣ This opens calcium channels in the β cell resulting in calmodulin activation, which in turn leads to insulin secretion.
