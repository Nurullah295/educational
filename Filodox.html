Disease profile:
Asthma:
It is a long-term inflammatory disease of the airways of the lungs. It is characterized by variable symptoms such as wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness & cough.
Wheezing:
This is a continuous, coarse, whistling sound produced in the respiratory airways during breathing
Shortness of breath:
It is known medically as dyspnea - is often described as an intense tightening in the chest, air hunger, difficulty breathing, breathlessness or a feeling of suffocation.
COPD:
It is a chronic inflammatory lung disease that causes obstructed airflow from the lungs. Symptoms include -breathing difficulty, cough, mucoid sputum production and wheezing. It includes chronic bronchitis & emphysema.
Exercise-induced bronchoconstriction:
It is the narrowing of the airways causing difficulty moving air out of the lungs during exercise or physical activity.
Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)
1. Why is Filodox superb drug for asthma management?
Ans: Asthma is a long term inflammatory disease. Filodox effectively inhibits phosphodiesterase enzyme and platelet activating factor thus controls asthma effectively. Moreover, it offers better safety option than Theophylline or Amynophylline.
2. Why is Doxofylline better than Theophylline or Amynophylline?
Ans: Theophylline & Aminophylline have more affinity for Adenosine A1 & A2 receptor but Doxofylline has very less affinity for Adenosine receptor. As a result chances of side effects like palpitation, tachycardia are very low with Doxofylline.
3. Why Doxofylline is safe for heart?
Ans: Doxofylline is safe for heart because it has less affinity towards Adenosine 1 and Adenosine 2 receptors thus it doesn't increase heart rate and decrease arithmetic effect.
4. What are the contraindications of Doxofylline?
Ans: Doxofylline is contraindicated in-
• Acute myocardial infarction Patients with hypotension
• Lactating mother
• Hypersensitivity to its component
5. When Doxofylline should be taken?
Ans: It should be taken at the same time each day, preferably in the evening with food.

6. What is ACOS?
Ans: "Asthma COPD Overlap Syndrome" (ACOS) has been applied to the condition in which a person has clinical feature of both asthma and COPD.
