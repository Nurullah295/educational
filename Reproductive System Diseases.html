Reproductive System Diseases

1. Contraception: It is a method or device used to prevent pregnancy.
2. Dysmenorrhoea: Dysmenorrhoea is the medical term for painful menstrual periods which are caused by uterine contractions. Primary dysmenorrhoea is common and refers to recurrent pain with no underlying diseases, while secondary dysmenorrhoea results from reproductive system disorders.
3. Dyspareunia: Dyspareunia is the term for recurring pain in the genital area or within the pelvis during sexual intercourse.
4. Genitourinary syndrome of menopause (GSM): Genitourinary syndrome of menopause (GSM) is a chronic, progressive condition of vulvo-vaginal & urinary tract due to lack of estrogen during and after menopause. The most common sign symptoms of GSM include- vaginal dryness, dyspareunia, irritation, burning, dysuria, urgency, repeated UTI, pelvic pain, vaginal vault prolapse, urine incontinence.
5. Hot Flash: A hot flash is the sudden feeling of warmth in the upper body, which is usually most intense over the face, neck and chest.
6. Hypoactive Sexual Desire Disorder (HSDD): HSDD is defined as the absence of sexual fantasies and thoughts, and/or desire for or receptivity to, sexual activity that causes the personal distress or difficulties in her relationship.
7. Hypomagnesemia: It is an electrolyte disturbance caused when there is a low level of serum magnesium (less than 1.46 mg/dL) in the blood.
8. Low Birth Weight: Is a term used to describe babies who are born weighing less than 5 pounds, 8 ounces (2500 g).
9. Menopause: The time when a woman's ovaries stop producing hormones and menstrual periods stop. Natural menopause usually occurs around age 50.
10. Postmenopausal women: Women are considered to be postmenopausal when they have not had their period for an entire year.
11. Spontaneous Abortion: It's the loss of pregnancy naturally before 20 weeks of gestation. It is also referred to as miscarriage.
