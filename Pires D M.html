Product Orientation & Presentation:
Cough:
☞ A cough is simply a reflex that tries to remove the irritant and to clear the air way.
Coughing starts when part of your breathing passage becomes inflamed or irritated by matter such as dust, smoke or mucus.

Types of Cough:
☞ Broadly there are two main types of cough:

Dry (Unproductive coughs) which produces no mucus or phlegm, which can be described as ‘dry’ or ‘tickly’

Mucus (Productive) coughs which produces excess mucus or phlegm, which can be described as ‘mucus’ or ‘chesty’

The treatment chosen depends on whether the cough is productive or nonproductive.
Martindale 39th, p-1684

Acute, Sub-acute and Chronic cough:
☞ Based on the duration, it is also classified into acute, sub-acute and chronic cough:

Acute coughs generally last for less than three weeks.

Sub-Acute coughs lasting for three to eight weeks.

Chronic or persistent coughs lasting for more than 8 weeks.

New England Journal of Medicine; 2000, V-343:23, p-1715
