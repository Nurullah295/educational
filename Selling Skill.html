Two Basic Approaches in Business Development
☆ Product development through Product-wise doctor selection
• If you are searching right doctors for achieving the target and establishing Glix tablet, then you are following this approach. Here product Glix is fixed, but doctors are variable.
☆ Doctor development through Doctor-wise product selection
• If are trying to develop Dr. X as an Advocate of Navana through selecting/prescribing products at you choice, then you are following this approach. Here doctor X is fixed, but products are variable.

Activities in the Selling Process (Product Development):
1. Prospecting :
Identify potential prescriber of your products
2. Classify Suspects and Qualifying:
Categorize doctors based on their potentiality and possibility of prescribing your product
3. Develop a Selling Strategy and Call Objectives:
Set the basic strategy for generating prescriptions from the prospect and objective of the first call by matching a key need to a product benefit.
4. Make the approach:
Gain the attention and interest of the prospect using the need benefit match selected above.
5. Make the presentation:
Expand on the product benefits to satisfy needs and checking for objections.
6. Trial Close:
Test close on a minor point to see if the prospect is accepting the benefits and is ready to prescribe.
7. Handling objections:
Accept objections as form of feedback. Clarify needs and provide proof that the product benefits will meet the needs.
8. Conclude presentation and close:
When a sufficient number of benefits have been accepted, the sales person will move to close, which is basically asking for prescription.
9. Follow-up:
Collect feedback from doctors, ensure availability of the product and convert the doctor as Repeat Customer.

• Brand loyal:
If a doctor is strongly loyal (almost 100% support) to a brand, then you have to find out the background information before trying to shift the brand.
• Company loyal:
There are few doctors who prefer to prescribe almost all the products of a specific company. In this case, you should give priority of those products that are not available with that company.
• Brand shifter:
These are the doctors who frequently shift brands. Be careful while taking commitment For SPS. Here you can for Therapeutic Category commitment e.g. he or she will prescribe NPL brands of anti-ulcerant rather than for Esotac or Rabetac or Ometac.

Step-7: Handling objections
• Accept objections as form of feedback.
• Clarify needs and provide proof that the product benefits will meet the needs.

Handling an objection is an integral part of the selling process.
• Rarely can you find sales taking place without objections.
• Objections are really the response to the sales presentation. It shows us that the prospect has been touched emotionally.
• Objections are not rejections and should not be taken personally.
• To a well-prepared professional, every objection is an opportunity to close.
• On hearing an objection, a good professioiial feels encouraged because this is his opportunity to convince and use his time-tested principles of selling.
• All Objections and Responses Can and Should Be Anticipated
• A good professional should be prepared in advanceto prevent objections.

Objections, Conditions and Excuses
Objection: Related to FAB
• When a buyer raises an objection he is really saying, ‘I am not convinced yet. If I get convinced, probably I will prescribe your product.
• Example: Your claim of 100% success rate is not convincing.

A condition is a circunistance that cannot be changed and prevents the buyer from making a purchase.
• No matter how good a salesperson I am, a condition cannot be changed and I cannot sell.
• Example: Until and unless your product is available in Govt. supply, I cannot prescribe. (raised by Indoor doctor).

Excuses related to hidden objection
• Sometimes, a resistance is voiced and sometimes it’s not. The worst kind of objection one has is the one that is not brought out in the open, or hidden resistance — the one that actually prevents the doctor from prescribing your products, but which is not verbalized. Some hidden objections maybe:
‣ Sometimes, a Chemist shop (or Clinic) based doctor could not prescribe a product until the Chemist (or owner of the clinic) gives a signal.
‣ The doctor is committed to give 100% support to another brand.
‣ The doctor is annoyed with previous TMO’s behavior
‣ The doctor is not convinced about the quality of the product

In case of hidden objections, The most important thing is to find out the real objection.
• Whenever there is a hesitation from the prospect (doctor), it is very crucial to understand what is going through his mind. One needs to probe further, tactfully.
• Some examples of probing questions are:
‣ My AM/RM Sir would like to visit you on next Saturday? Would you please allow him to discuss about Esotac?
‣ Dear Sir/Madam, you have lot of scope to prescribe Esomeprazole. But I am getting not a single Rx of our Esotac. Would you please tell me what goes wrong with Esotac? (before asking this question, be sure that the doctor is not committed 100% to any brand).
‣ It would be a great honor for us to have Rx of Esotac from you. Would you please give me an opportunity?

Objection-handling process:
• Listen
• Question
◦ Confirm the objection
◦ Isolate the objection
• Think
• Handle
• Check
• Then go for the close

Listen
• Stop! Do not try to jump in at the beginning - this may cause further objection. When you interrupt them, you are objecting to their objection. If you refuse to listen, then their next steps may well be towards the door.
• Use active listening methods, nodding and physically showing interest.
• They are trying to tell you something that will help you sell to them, which is a gift from them to you. If you do not listen, then their next step may well be towards the door.

Question
• As appropriate, ask some questions. This not only shows you are interested in them, but it also gives you more information with which to make the sale. As you question them, watch carefully for body language that gives you more information about what they are thinking and feeling.
• Remember that this is not an interrogation, and that giving them the ‘third degree’ will turn them off. So keep your questions light and relevant.
• You might also tip the bucket at this time, asking them if there are any more concerns (=objections) that they have, and which, if you can resolve them, you might gain a close.
• It is not always necessary to ask questions.

Think
• Now before you dive into objection-handling, think!
• What methods will work best with them?
• Should you take a direct and confrontational approach or should you use the soft-soap to finesse the situation?
• Or maybe you should put it off to another day (but only if you can be sure that you can return to the selling situation).
• Thinking is a good thing where you are adding a little pause into the proceedings, thus demonstrating how you are taking their objection seriously.

Handle
• This stage may sometimes only be a few seconds after they object or it may require more time in the previous three steps.
• Now, when you are ready, use the objection-handling method that you believe will work best. Or make up your own.
• You are under no obligation to try and force-fit a method where it is unlikely to work.

Check
• Finally, check to find out whether your objection-handling worked!
• Ask if you have answered their question.
• Ask if there are any more concerns.
• As necessary, handle outstanding objections.
• Then go for the close.

Step-8: Conclude presentation and close:
When a sufticient number of benefits have been accepted. the sales person will to move to close on the goal of the call, which may he a demonstration or the order.

Closing
• A closing is a natural outcome of a good presentation.
• Selling can be a lot like fly fishing.
• If you tug hard on the line. it will snap and the fish will get away.
• The best method is a gentle coaxing that gradually brings the fish in to shore - although sometimes when they are spooked you have to let them out again and calm them down further away.

ABC
• ‘ABC’ is a common abbreviation: Always Be Closing.
• It means that you should always be heading towards a close, although you must also be careful about over-doing this.
• If people are nowhere near ready to buy, this will just annoy them.

Assumptive Close
Technique
• Act as if the other person has made the decision already.
• Turn the focus of the conversation towards the next level of questions, such as how many they want, when they want it delivered, what size they need, and so on.
Examples
◦ Would you please give an order of 10 boxes today? (applicable ONLY for DCC or PC)
◦ Would you please recommend your Pharmacy to place order? (applicable ONLY for hospitals or clinics where pharmacy places order on recommendation of the indoor doctors)

Calendar Close
Technique
• If they are not ready to close now, agree a future date when you can meet to discuss further This at least keeps the deal alive and you return to fight another day. It may also be just what you are seeking to take you to the next step in the deal strategy.
• Putting dates in the diary may also be an assumptive method, assuming that closure has taken place and that it is just a matter of when.
Examples
◦ I will bring all rferences on next Sunday to explain the mechanism of action in detail.

Compliment Close
Technique
• Be nice to them. Tell them how wonderful they are. Be amazed and impressed by them.
• Cast them as the expert so they sell to themselves. Tell them how good. they look or sound.
• Tell them how others will be impressed by them. Tell them how impressed you are with them as a person. Admire their integrity.
• Then ask for the sale.
Examples
◦ Dear Sir/Madam, ONLY you have the scope to prescribe such a sophisticated drug. I have made the product available in nearby pharmacies considering your scope. May I expect the 1st prescription from you?
For complicated UTI, patients reply on you and our drug can cure the complicated UTI. So may I expect prescription in favor of X from next available patient?

Conditional close
Technique
• When the other person offers an objection, make it a condition of resolving their objection that they make the purchase.
• Always, by the way, phrase it in the form ‘If I...will you...’ rather than ‘Will you..if I...’. This is because our brains work very quickly and starting with ‘will you’ causes them to begin thinking immediately about objections and they may miss the exchange. On the other hand, starting with If I...’ will cause psychological closure on what you are offering thus drawing them in to the close.
Examples
• As you suggested us to launch Esotac capsule, we are going to introduce Esotac in Capsule form in this week. May I request...... prescription per day in favor of our Esotac capsule?
• If we change the presentation of our......product, may I have your Rx support in favor of our........ ? • If I can justify the 100% efficacy of.....in complicated UTI, may I expect your100% support for...... in case of complicated UTI?

Demonstration Close
Technique
• if you are able to, do a spectacular demonstration of your product that really makes them ‘wow’.
• If you can’t do it with them there (which is by far and away the best), then use a video or otherwise add impact.
• Give them a go. Let them try and find out for themselves.
Examples
• if you allow me. I would like to request you to have a taste of our chewcee. I am confident that you will find it as the best in term of taste. (.... After completion of Demo---- )May I request------Rx in favor of Chewcee?
• You can see (showing the accessories of product) we have provided spoon, measuring cup and dropper in our....... for the conveniences of your patients in all age groups. May I request----Rx in favor of------?

Emotion Close
Technique
• Play to their emotions. deliberately evoking specific emotions.
• Find if they respond more to positive or negative emotions and act accordingly.
• If in doubt, go for positive emotions - these are usually better.
Examples
• Today we are celebrating Esotac Mother’s Day. I have a target of 20 Rx today. Without yur support I could not achieve this target. Would you please give me at least 10 Rx for Esotac today?

Summary Close
Technique
• Summarize the list of benefits that the other person will receive, telling them the full extent of what they are getting for their money.

The psychology of relationships in selling
• A quality relationship is built with a solid foundation.
• In the foundation, there are four major bricks.
‣ Credibility
‣ Trust
‣ Common Ground
‣ Communication

The Credibility Brick
• Credibility is the basis for how new relationships are started in the sales process.
• Credibility begins with you understanding your potential customer, their situation, market and possible struggles.
• Being credible is a process not a single event.
• It is a practice of consistency.
• it takes time to build credibility but only a short while to break it down.

The Trust Brick
• Trust and credbitity are often confused. They are mutually exclusive, but codependent in nature.
• The earlier you build trust the quicker you’ll move the sale forward.
• As with credibility, trust is not an event but a process.
• You develop it with each correspondence you have either verbally or written.
• Sales are often lost due to a lack of trust felt by the buyer.

The Common Ground Brick
• Relationships happen because there is a connection. in most cases, connections are made because two people share something in common.
• Your responsibility as a sales professional is to find that commonality. The quicker you can find the common bond the sooner you’ll solidify the relationship.
• Common ground usually starts with basic things like children, sports, activities, special interests, etc.
• Be cautious to find common ground with the taboo topics like religion or politics.
• “Must one have common ground to make a sale?” The answer is, No. However, if you want to build and maintain long-term relationships, developing a common ground bond is an important key.
