Musculoskeletal Diseases

1. Ankylosing Spondylitis:
Is a type of arthritis that primarily affects the spinal joints along with other joints which leads to severe chronic pain, inflammation and discomfort.

2. Bisphosphonate:
Bisphosphonates are a group of drugs that inhibit osteoclasts which are responsible for breaking down and reabsorbing minerals such as calcium from bone.

3 BMD (Bone Mineral Density):
Bone density or bone mineral density, is the amount of bone mineral in bone tissue.

4 Joint Stiffness:
Joint stiffness is the feeling that the motion of a joint is limited or difficult and loses flexibility over time. Joint stiffness can be caused by inflammation in the synovium, the lining of the joint.

5. Osteoarthritis:
(Osteoarthritis (OA) is a degenerative joint disease that causes the breakdown of cartilage and bone in the joints.)
It most commonly affects joints in knees, hips, arms and spine, in which the joints cartilage wears down over time, resulting in joint pain, stiffness, swelling and loss of flexibility.

6. Osteomalacia:
Is a disease that weakens bones and can cause them to break more easily.(It's also known as adult rickets.)

7. Osteoporosis:
It means "porous bone." It is a bone disease that occurs when the body loses too much bone, makes too little bone or both.

8. Postmenopausal Osteoporosis:
Postmenopausal osteoporosis generally develops after menopause, when estrogen levels drop.

9. Rheumatoid Arthritis:
It is a chronic inflammatory disorder, where the body's autoimmune system attacks the joints leading to tender, swollen joints and joint stiffness.

10. Rickets:
It is the softening and weakening of bones in children.

11. Spasticity:
(Spasticity is a condition that causes muscles to feel stiff or tight, and can make it difficult to move, walk, or speak.)
Spasticity is a condition in which muscles stiffen or tighten, preventing normal fluid movement. The muscles remain contracted and resist being stretched, thus affecting movement, speech and gait.
